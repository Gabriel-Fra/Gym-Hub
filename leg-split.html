Leg Split

Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves — 2–3 days per week

The Leg Split dedicates full sessions to lower body development — the largest muscle group in the body. Training legs hard drives systemic muscle growth, boosts testosterone output, and builds the athletic base that supports every other lift. This split separates quad-dominant and hip-dominant sessions to allow you to train each pattern with full intensity rather than splitting focus in a single session.

Programming Tips

  • Never skip leg day — lower body training drives whole-body hormonal response and size.
  • Allow 48–72 hours between leg sessions; they're systemically taxing.
  • Prioritise squat depth over load. Poor depth means less muscle, not more strength.
  • Calves need high volume and frequency to grow — hit them every leg session.
